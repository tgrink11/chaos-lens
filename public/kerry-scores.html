Where the data comes from

Every weekday at 6:00 pm ET — about two hours after the US market close — a scheduled job pulls the latest ticker lists from Kerry's Google Sheet (Tell Sheet and Watchlist) and fetches two years of daily OHLCV data for each symbol. That price history is then run through the chaos-lens fractal engine, and the results land in the table below. No human intervention is required; the page you're looking at is the unedited model output.

What the engine measures

Three fractal metrics describe the shape of recent price action — not the direction, just how the chart looks geometrically. The engine pairs them with a momentum/behavioral layer (recent return, candle wicks, volume spikes) to translate shape into a directional read.

  • Hurst (H) — persistence. H > 0.55 means the price is trending (moves in the same direction longer than chance). H ≈ 0.5 is a random walk (no edge). H < 0.45 is mean-reverting (every move tends to reverse). Note: H measures persistence, not bullish/bearish — a strong downtrend has high H too.
  • Box dimension (D) — smoothness. D close to 1 is a clean line (steady trend). D near 1.5 is typical market chop. D near 2 is space-filling chaos (frantic noise). Low D plus a recent up-move suggests a breakout is building; high D suggests the structure is breaking down.
  • Lacunarity (Λ) — gap distribution. Λ > 1.4 means buying or selling is clustered into pockets (accumulation/distribution). Λ close to 1 means uniform grinding with no concentration. High Λ often precedes directional moves once the clusters resolve.

How to read each column

Symbol / List — Ticker and which list it came from (Tell Sheet or Watchlist). HUT and MRVL appear on both lists; only one row is stored, tagged with whichever list was scored last.
Price — Most recent daily close from the two-year history pulled at scan time. Not a real-time quote.
15-Day / 62-Day — Directional outlook for the next ~3 and ~13 trading weeks, derived from horizon-matched analog windows (the engine searches historical periods with similar fractal signatures and looks at what happened 15 or 62 bars later). Bullish / bearish / neutral.
15d / 62d Conf — How concentrated the bullish/bearish vote is. 90% is the cap; anything >70% means most signals agree. <50% means the model is uncertain.
Prediction — The engine's single "next-break" call. Thrust Up expects a breakout higher; Cascade Down expects a sharp decline; Consolidation means no clear direction — likely chop.
Pred Conf — How dominant the winning call was vs. the runners-up. >60% is a high-conviction signal; <40% means competing interpretations.
Top Reason — The highest-weighted signal that drove the prediction. Hover for the full text. Examples: "Hurst 0.66 persistent + uptrend", "Box dimension 1.12 — smooth decline", "Volume spike on decline — capitulation risk".
Mood — Market psychology label. Euphoria = trending up with healthy structure; Panic = breakdown plus high fear; Stealth Build = quiet accumulation pattern, often precedes a move up; Grind = directionless chop.
H / D / Λ — Raw fractal values explained above. Useful for spotting your own setups (e.g. sort by H descending to find the strongest persistence; sort by D ascending to find the smoothest paths).
Report — Opens the full chaos-lens analysis for that ticker in a new tab: multi-timeframe fractals, behavioral signals, historical analogs, an AI narrative, and a walk-forward backtest.

How to use the table

  • Click any column header to sort. Numeric columns sort descending by default; click again to flip.
  • Use the Tell Sheet / Watchlist pills to filter to one list, or type a symbol in the search box.
  • Look for agreement across columns: a stock with bullish 15-day + bullish 62-day + Thrust Up + Euphoria + low D is a much higher-conviction setup than one where the signals disagree.
  • Be cautious of divergences: bullish horizons with a Cascade Down prediction (or vice-versa) means the longer trend and the near-term micro-structure are pulling in opposite directions — the prediction tends to lead in the short term.
  • This is one model's read on a snapshot of price data. It is not investment advice. Use it as a screening layer, not a decision rule.
